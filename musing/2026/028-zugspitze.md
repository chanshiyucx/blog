Zugspitze is called *the Top of Germany*, and it's at an elevation of 2,962 meters. We had planned this hike for a long time. Eventually, summer finally came, so we could make it happen.

We purchased two tickets offline, and they cost about 78 euros per person. It was a little expensive, but I think it was worth it. The whole trip included four steps. First, we took a cogwheel train through a four-kilometer tunnel from Eibsee to the sightseeing platform. We took a rest and had a simple lunch at a vegan restaurant. The ice hadn't melted, and the scenery was fantastic. Then we took a cable car to the summit of Zugspitze. From the top, the Eibsee looked like a blue gemstone on the ground. In the end, we took a cable car back to the Eibsee. I took lots of photos with my camera, and it became an unforgettable memory for me.
